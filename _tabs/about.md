Senthil Siva writes about security, authorization, AI agents, and the systems challenges that come with powerful but untrusted components.

His focus is on **structural** approaches: capability-based authorization, attenuable tokens, runtime boundaries, and designs where certain classes of harm become unrepresentable rather than merely unlikely. He is the author of [Warden](https://github.com/senthil1216/attenuate-agent), a Rust-based reference runtime that enforces strict, auditable authority limits on agent tool calls.

This site is the home for longer-form technical writing on these topics and related work in Rust, infrastructure, and application security.

- **Current work**: Engineering leader focused on cloud platforms, security, and agentic systems (Bengaluru)

Previously he has worked across Oracle SaaS/PaaS and modern cloud infrastructure.
